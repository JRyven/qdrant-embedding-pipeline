---
title: Sonnet - Winter by the Harbor
date: 2025-12-31

Winter hush: the harbor breathes in foam,
A low and patient swell that knows the moon;
The mast is memory, the hull a home
That rocks to slow and distant seaside tune.
A gull tugs at the last wet ribboned light,
The tide lays down a glass of brittle air;
Grey cloud unbuttons day and gulps the white
Of wave and lip and brine across the pier.
Wind laces ice with threads of scattered spray,
The keel remembers summer's easy sun;
Now lantern smoke and salted ribs convey
The rough rejoinder winter sets begun.
And in the swell a softened thunder rolls —
The ocean keeps its ledger of our souls.
